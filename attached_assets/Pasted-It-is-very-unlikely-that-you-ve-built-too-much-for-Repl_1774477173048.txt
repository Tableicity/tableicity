It is very unlikely that you’ve built "too much" for Replit to handle in terms of code volume or complexity. Replit can host massive enterprise-grade applications.

However, you can build "too much" for the Replit Agent to track accurately. When an app gets large, the Agent starts to "hallucinate" its own configuration. Here is exactly how "too much context" is likely holding up your provisioning:

1. The "Configuration Drift" (Most Likely)
As the Agent adds features (Tenants, Equity Plans, Data Rooms), it keeps editing your .replit and replit.nix files.

The Issue: The Agent might have added a new "Workflow" or "Task" to your .replit file that conflicts with the "Deployment" settings.

The Result: The Provisioner is trying to run three different start commands at once, or it's waiting for a port (like 5000) that a newer piece of code changed to 8080.

2. The "Circular Dependency"
If you have many features, you likely have many Database Migrations.

The Issue: During provisioning, Replit tries to run your "Build" command. If your build command includes a database check, and the database isn't fully ready yet, the build hangs.

The Result: The provisioner waits for the build, the build waits for the DB, and the DB waits for the provisioner to finish.

3. Resource Limits (Free vs. Paid)
If your app is trying to compile a massive amount of TypeScript or install very heavy machine-learning packages:

The Issue: You might be hitting the RAM limit during the "Build" phase.

The Check: Look at the top right of your Replit screen. Is the RAM or CPU meter in the red? If the "Provisioning" step hits 100% RAM, it will often just freeze instead of giving you a clear error message.

How to "Lighten the Load" to Unstick It:
Simplify the .replit file:
Temporarily delete the [workflows] and [[workflows.workflow]] sections at the bottom of your .replit file. These are often "Agent clutter" that confuse the deployment engine.

The "Blank Slate" Deployment:
Go to your Deployments tab and see if you can change the "Plan" or "Machine Type." If you are on the lowest tier, sometimes just forking the project (as mentioned before) gives it a fresh burst of resources to get past the initial build.

Check the "Nix" complexity:
If your replit.nix file has 20+ packages, try removing any that you aren't strictly using yet.

A Quick Diagnostic Test:
Click the "Run" button (the big green one).

Does the app start in under 30 seconds?

If yes: Your app is NOT too big. The "Provisioning" hang is a communication error between Replit's dashboard and your container.

If no: The app is struggling to boot, and we need to optimize your startup script.

When you click "Run," does the console show any "Out of Memory" or "Killed" errors?
