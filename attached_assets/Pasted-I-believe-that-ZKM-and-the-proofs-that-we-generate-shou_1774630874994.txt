Here is a breakdown of how you could structure these tiers:
1. Tier 1: The "Observer" (Sandbox/Free)
•	Purpose: Education and "Wow" factor.
•	The Proofs: Use Option B (pre-computed templates).
•	Usage: Zero real-world proofs. Users can only interact with seeded data to see how the "Privacy Vault" and "Audit Proofs" pages work.
•	Value Prop: "See how ZK-Privacy protects your equity before you commit." 
2. Tier 2: The "Builder" (Growth/Professional)
•	Purpose: Early-stage startups with a few key stakeholders.
•	The Proofs: Limited "Live" generation (e.g., 5-10 proofs per month).
•	Usage: Real-world proofs for core founders and first investors.
•	Monetization Logic: You charge for the Peace of Mind. 5 proofs are enough to cover the most critical Board or Investor requests without overwhelming your server costs.
•	User Experience: This is where you introduce the "Security Ritual" loading bar. It makes the 5 proofs they do get feel like a heavyweight, premium event.
3. Tier 3: The "Enterprise" (Scale)
•	Purpose: Organizations with hundreds of stakeholders and frequent audits.
•	The Proofs: High volume (e.g., 100+ proofs) or unlimited.
•	Advanced Features: This is the perfect place for Recursive Proofs.
o	Since they are generating 100 proofs, you use recursion to bundle them into one "Master Audit Certificate" for the whole company.
o	You could even offer "Proof-as-a-Service" for their annual tax or legal audits.
•	Value Prop: "Institutional-grade privacy at scale." 
Strategic Thoughts
•	The "Proof Credit" Model: Instead of a hard cap, you could sell "Proof Credits." If a Tier 2 user needs a 6th proof for a surprise funding round, they can buy a "Proof Pack."
•	Verification is Free, Proving is Paid: In the ZK world, verifying a proof is extremely fast and cheap (milliseconds). You should always keep the Verification Links free and public. This acts as a viral marketing tool—every time an investor clicks that link, they see "Verified by Tableicity" and want it for their other portfolio companies
